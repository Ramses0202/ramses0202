Japanese culture

Japan's culture is the result of a historical process that began with the immigration waves originating from the Asian continent and the islands of the Pacific Ocean, followed by a strong cultural influence from China. Subsequently, the Tokugawa shogunate established a long period of isolation from the rest of the world (sakoku) in the middle of the 17th century, which lasted until the beginning of the Meiji Era, at the end of the 19th century, when it received an immense foreign influence that increased after the end of the Second World War. This in effect resulted in a culture distinct from other Asian cultures. In Japan, personal interrelationships are strongly influenced by the ideas of "duty", "honor" and "obligation", a set known as giri (義理?), And which represents a custom different from the individualistic culture of Western nations. The concepts of "desirable behaviors" and "morality" are less practiced in family, school and friendship situations; however, a more formal practice is observed in front of superiors or unknown people. One aspect that has played a significant role in Japanese culture is the Japanese language. Nemawashi (根 回 し?) Is also practiced, which refers to the careful and serious preparation of a project, which reflects the accepted and respected harmony within Japanese culture. On the other hand, the Japanese possess an intricate and complicated sense of humor, which is highly reflected in culture, ethics, language, and religion, and which is sometimes considered to be very difficult to interpret by other cultures.
